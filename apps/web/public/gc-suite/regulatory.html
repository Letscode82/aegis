Breach notification windows collide CERT-In 6hDPDP 24hGDPR 72h

The same incident on the payments app can trigger three clocks. CERT-In's 6-hour reporting (cyber incident) is stricter than DPDP's 24h (personal data breach) and GDPR's 72h — but the report contents and recipients differ, so "comply to strictest" alone is not sufficient.

Cartographer proposal: harmonize CTRL-042 to a single 6h internal clock with three jurisdiction-specific notification templates branching from one runbook. One control, three obligations satisfied. → Awaiting CISO approval.

Localization vs. transfer RBI payments data IN-onlyGDPR Ch.V transfers

RBI requires payments data stored only in India; the EU branch's support tooling replicates ticket payloads (containing payment metadata) to Frankfurt. Genuine collision — no single control satisfies both as architected.

Cartographer proposal: data-minimised ticket payloads (strip payment fields before replication) + SCCs for the remainder. Flagged to Architecture board — this one needs a human decision, and the graph says exactly why.
